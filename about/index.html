The short version

I'm Jacob Dart — Jake to everyone in town.

I live in Pocatello, and I help local businesses take the repetitive work off their week — the re-typing, the follow-up, the chasing. I find the thing eating your hours, set up something that handles it, and then teach you until you own it without me.

I didn't get here from a consulting deck. I got here by drowning in my own busywork and fixing it — tools that take notes for me, remember everything I've done, and handle the repeat work on their own. Once my own week came back, I realized that's the thing worth offering a neighbor. Not advice. The working setup.

What I believe

  • You should own what you pay for. No lock-in, no black box — it lives on your accounts.
  • Never automate a broken process. Fix the way you do it now, then hand the clean version to AI.
  • If it won't pay for itself, don't buy it. If a build won't pay for itself several times over, I'll tell you not to do it — even when that costs me the sale.
  • Fewer tools, not more. The goal is a quieter, steadier business — not a longer software bill.
  • Build to completion. We're done when it runs without me, not when the call ends.
